About Me

I'm a robotics graduate student at the University of Maryland (UMD) having expereince working in computer vision, robotics software, reinforcement learning, and multimodal AI.

Currently, I'm a research assistant at the Gamma Lab, UMD headed by Dr. Dinesh Manocha where I research path planning technqiues for autonomous systems. I've also previously interned at OmniVision where I worked with event camera-assisted 3D reconstriction and spatial video generation. Apart from these experiences, I've also worked at the MarMot Lab, National University of Singapore, MOIR Lab, IIT Ropar, and Aspiro, Bangalore where I was involved in similar research and projects.

I also work on various personal and academic projects in these areas and open-source my work on GitHub. I enjoy writing and frequently publish technical blogs on Medium as well.

In my free time, I like playing badminton, table tennis, and swimming. I also enjoy going on hikes and exploring new places. I've also recently gotten into videography! During my time at IIT Ropar, I was an active and core member of the Debating Society (DebSoc) and represented my university at numerous competitions.
